Key Insights

  • Net business creation dropped from 3,566 in Q1 2024 to 456 in Q1 2025 across regions with complete data, despite openings increasing slightly.
  • Top net-opening regions in Q1 2025 were British Columbia (592), Alberta (510), and Quebec (315).
  • Highest closure pressure (close/open ratio) appeared in Prince Edward Island (1.062), Nova Scotia (1.027), and Yukon (1.027).
  • Most improved net performance came from British Columbia (266), followed by Manitoba (172) and Northwest Territories (4).
  • Largest decline in absolute net openings was concentrated in Ontario, which is material because of its overall business base size.

Recommendations

  • Prioritize retention-focused support in high-closure-pressure regions: cash-flow coaching in first 12 months, milestone-based mentoring, and early-warning triggers for liquidity stress.
  • Scale growth programs in high-net regions (especially Western provinces) using sector-specific accelerator cohorts and faster micro-financing approvals.
  • Create an Ontario stabilization initiative combining market validation sprints, procurement-readiness training, and post-launch advisor check-ins.
  • Deploy a monthly regional risk dashboard in Power BI/Tableau using the output files in outputs/ to identify where closure growth outpaces opening growth.
Data quality note: suppressed/unavailable records detected for Nunavut; excluded from aggregate comparisons.
